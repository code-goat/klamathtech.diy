# Your Data Is Not Free

Every time you open a browser, send a text, or search for something online, you leave a trail. Who reads that trail? What do they do with it?

Most of us have never been told. We were handed phones and accounts and apps without a manual. This guide series is that manual — written for neighbors, elders, young people, and anyone who wants to take back a little control over their digital life.

You do not need to be a tech person. You just need to care about yourself and your community.

---

## Why Privacy Matters Here

Privacy is not just about hiding things. It is about **power** — specifically, who has it over you.

When a corporation collects your location, your searches, your messages, and your browsing habits, they build a detailed profile of who you are. That profile gets sold to advertisers, shared with law enforcement, and used to predict and influence your behavior. This is not paranoia — it is their publicly documented business model.

For communities in the Klamath bioregion, the stakes are especially real:

- **Elder residents** are disproportionately targeted by phone and online scams, many of which use personal data purchased from data brokers to seem legitimate
- **Tribal members and activists** have documented histories of government and corporate surveillance targeting Indigenous-led movements
- **Rural households** often rely on limited internet providers with little competition — and those providers legally sell your browsing history
- **Everyone** faces the everyday harm of being profiled, manipulated, and exploited by systems built to extract value from their attention and data

Taking digital privacy seriously is an act of **community self-defense**.

## A Few Things to Keep in Mind

**You don't have to do everything at once.** Privacy is a practice, not a destination. Even one change — switching your browser, setting up a password manager — meaningfully improves your situation. Pick what feels manageable and start there.

**Perfect is the enemy of good.** You will never be completely private online. That's okay. The goal is to make yourself a much harder target than you are now, and to stop being a passive product of surveillance systems you never agreed to.

**This is not about fear — it's about agency.** We believe technology should serve people, not exploit them. These guides are about taking back a little control, building your own knowledge, and helping the people around you do the same.
